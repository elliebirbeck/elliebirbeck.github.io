PROJECT / StressLess / Dec 16

Over two months I was part of a team of five students to create an interactive stress ball. StressLess is a working prototype of a product which innovates on the standard stress ball by incorporating pressure sensors and connectivity.

A range of metrics are collected as a user interacts with the ball, and these can be utilised in a variety of ways through use of our API. Examples of use-cases we envisioned include responding to the data by playing relaxing music, or controlling ambient lighting. The video below demonstrates a colour-changing visualisation we developed for a pilot experiment, which fades and slows at a frequency slightly below the user's own squeezing rate, encouraging them to reduce it.

The whole team was responsible for the high-level design of the product and system, and my particular role in the implementation was hardware development of the physical device. We wrote a paper which details related work, the design process, implementation, and the results of our user study.
